In an era of rapidly evolving HPC and AI workloads, traditional on-premises systems often fall short in delivering the agility, scalability, and cost-efficiency required to stay competitive. Energy Efficient Computing: Liquid Cooling in the Era of AI and HPC

Cold air has been the traditional means for removing heat and keeping data center systems cool. But air-cooling methods can’t keep pace with the high energy consumption and dense configurations of racks and components like central processing units (CPUs), graphics processing units (GPUs) and tensor processing units (TPUs) used for AI, machine learning, HPC and enterprise workloads. Sustainability goals further pressure companies to find better, more environmentally friendly and energy-efficient ways to cool their systems.

Modern liquid cooling methods like Lenovo Neptune® meet both demands by combining efficient heat extraction from IT equipment with capturing the energy for potential reuse. Further, Neptune® reduces coolant disposal costs because its liquid coolant is environmentally friendly, requiring no special disposal treatments.

This white paper explains the soaring demand for liquid cooling in more detail and presents a compelling case study in The Flatiron Institute’s use of Neptune® in its large HPC infrastructure containing extensive computational capability.
